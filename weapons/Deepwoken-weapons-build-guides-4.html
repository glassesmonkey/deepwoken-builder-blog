Why Heavy Weapons are the GOAT in Deepwoken

Heavy weapons are like that friend who always skips leg day but never misses arm day. Here's why they're awesome:

  • Raw Power: Hit harder than your mom when she found out you skipped school
  • Massive Range: Social distancing? More like social destruction-ing
  • Posture Damage: Make enemies wobble like they've had one too many at the tavern
  • Hyperarmor: Laugh in the face of interruptions (and probably everything else)

The Terrifying Trio of Heavy Weapons

1. Greataxes: The Tree Choppers

For when you want to cosplay as a lumberjack... but with enemies instead of trees.

  • Top Pick: Enforcer's Axe (75 HVY, LVL 6 - it's like they strapped a guillotine to a stick)
  • Pro Tip: Use the wide swing arc to hit multiple enemies. It's like bowling, but with axes!

2. Greatswords: The Anime Protagonist Special

Because nothing says "main character energy" like a sword bigger than you are.

  • Top Pick: First Light (75 HVY, 30% penetration - it's not compensating, it's just efficient)
  • Pro Tip: Master the thrust attacks for when you need to poke something really, really hard

3. Greathammers: The BONK Brigade

When you absolutely, positively need to flatten everything in your path.

  • Top Pick: Sovereign's Punishment (75 HVY, 20 STR - it's like wielding a small moon)
  • Pro Tip: Aim for the head... or don't. With this thing, it doesn't really matter where you hit

Heavy Weapon Mechanics: The Science of Smashing

Let's get nerdy for a second (don't worry, it's still the cool kind of nerdy):

  • Damage: Higher than Mount Everest (okay, not really, but you get the idea)
  • Swing Speed: Slower than your grandma's internet, but twice as impactful
  • Scaling: Loves HVY stats more than a bear loves honey
  • Posture Damage: Turn guard breaks into guard obliterations

Pro Strats for Heavy Weapon Users

  • Timing is Everything: You swing slow, so make every hit count
  • Use Your Range: Keep enemies at the tip of your weapon where they can't hit you
  • Master Positioning: Be where your enemy is going to be, not where they are
  • Embrace the Tank: With a heavy weapon, you ARE the shield
